A build experiment that turned an idea into a working studio and 10 videos in production.

What happens when you compress months of building into a single sprint?

About two months ago, I started exploring a simple idea:

Could the way we input information be redesigned for the next generation of users?

Traditional forms feel static and slow. I wanted to experiment with something more interactive — closer to how people actually think and make decisions.

That exploration led to building a swipe-based input system. But once the interaction worked, a bigger question appeared:

What if structured inputs could drive entire visual explanations automatically?

So I ran a build sprint.

In under 24 hours, I put together a working studio that can generate structured videos from scene definitions. Instead of editing timelines, the system uses scene templates, visual components, and data structures to render explanations programmatically.

The interesting part isn’t just the videos — it’s the workflow.

A single structured system can now produce multiple videos with consistent visuals, diagrams, and narrative flow. Once the structure exists, the output becomes repeatable.

Right now, I’m using the studio to produce a small interactive documentary series that walks through how an idea evolves from a simple input interface into a larger product system.

It’s still early and definitely experimental, but the sprint proved something important:

When the workflow is right, building complex things can happen much faster than we usually assume.

Next step: finishing the first batch of videos and sharing the process publicly.
