Most people don’t struggle with information.

They struggle with

decisions.

Every day we ask questions like:

Which phone should I buy?

How do I research a topic properly?

What AI prompt will give the best result?

Which option is actually better?

The internet gives us

infinite information

, but very little help in

structuring decisions

.

So I started building something called

ViaDecide — a Decision Engine.

Instead of jumping across multiple websites, tools, and tabs, what if decisions could be structured inside a single system?

That system could help you:

break down complex choices

organize research

compare real options

experiment with ideas

even learn through simulations and games

This is the thinking behind ViaDecide.

Right now the platform includes a collection of small “decision tools”:

⚗️

Alchemist

– turn messy thoughts into structured decisions

🧠

Memory Engine

– store and reuse research

🛒

ONDC Demo / Engine Deals

– explore real product choices

📚

StudyOS

– structured research workflow for students

🎮

HexWars & Multiplayer Quiz Experiments

– learning through interactive simulations

⚡

Prompt Alchemy

– craft better prompts for AI tools

Each tool explores a different way to

improve decision quality

.

We spend years learning subjects in school.

But almost no time learning

how to make better decisions

.

Better decisions can improve:

learning

buying choices

career paths

research

entrepreneurship

The goal of this project is simple:

Build tools that help people think better.

Over the next few months I’ll be sharing:

experiments with decision tools

interactive simulations

AI workflows for thinking

small web tools and multiplayer experiences

ideas about building a “decision OS”

If you’re curious about

thinking systems, decision tools, and experimental web products

, feel free to follow along.

You can explore the project here:
