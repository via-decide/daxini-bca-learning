Over the past few weeks I’ve been running a series of small builder experiments while working on ViaDecide.

The goal isn’t to build something perfect immediately.

The goal is to document experiments while they happen — ideas, systems, workflows, and the thinking behind them.

This is how ViaDecide Builder Experiments started.

Experiment 1 — Rethinking Surveys

The first episode explored a simple question:

What if filling surveys worked like swiping in an app?

Traditional forms are slow and boring.

Swipe interactions allow people to make quick decisions instead of typing long answers.

That idea became the starting point for the first experiment.

Builder Sprint Experiment

While exploring that idea, I ran a 24-hour builder sprint to see how far the concept could go.

Instead of stopping at the interaction idea, the experiment evolved into something bigger — building a system that could structure and generate content around decisions and workflows.

The Idea Behind This Series

Most startup stories are shared after things work.

This series documents the messy part:

• early experiments

• ideas evolving into systems

• testing workflows

• building tools quickly to explore possibilities

Some ideas will fail.

Some will evolve into something useful.

But documenting the process publicly helps others see how systems actually emerge from experiments.

What Comes Next

The next experiments will explore:

• interactive content systems

• builder workflows

• structured decision tools

• new formats for explaining ideas

ViaDecide is still early, but the goal is simple:

Build experiments → document them → learn publicly.
