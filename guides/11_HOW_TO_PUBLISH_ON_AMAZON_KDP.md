# How to Publish Technical Books on Amazon KDP

Building great software or architecture is only half the battle. If you don't document and distribute it, your knowledge is trapped.

Here is the exact framework and methodology used to publish **The Daxini Stack** (a 5-volume technical series) on Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing (KDP).

---

## 1. The Strategy: Why KDP?
Amazon KDP allows you to publish eBooks (Kindle) and Paperbacks (Print-on-Demand) globally with zero upfront cost. For technical architecture books, this is the fastest way to build authority and distribute knowledge as a "Digital Sovereign".

## 2. Formatting the Manuscript
Before uploading, your manuscript must be perfectly formatted:
- **Trim Size:** `6" x 9"` is the standard for technical non-fiction and textbooks.
- **Interior:** Black & White (No Bleed) is the most cost-efficient option for text-heavy books. Only use color if you have complex architectural diagrams that require it.
- **Paper:** White paper provides the best contrast for reading technical code blocks.

## 3. Pricing Strategy (The "Infrastructure" Premium)
Do not price your technical books like cheap fiction. Position them as high-value engineering manuals.
- **Kindle eBook:** Set to **$9.99**. This maximizes your royalties (you get the 70% tier instead of 35% on Amazon).
- **Paperback:** Set to **$24.99**. A physical technical manual has a high perceived value. 
- **KDP Select (Kindle Unlimited):** Enroll in this if you want to maximize your distribution and reach over direct sales. You get paid per page read.

## 4. Amazon Metadata & SEO
Your metadata is how engineers will find your book.

### Title and Subtitle
Be highly specific. Don't use vague titles.
* **Example Title:** The Daxini Stack
* **Example Subtitle:** A Complete Blueprint for Building Sovereign AI Infrastructure, Local-First Apps, and Autonomous Governance Systems

### 7 Backend Keywords
Amazon gives you 7 backend keyword slots. Do not waste them on single words. Use long-tail phrases that engineers actually search for:
1. `Local-first software architecture`
2. `Sovereign AI systems design`
3. `Run local LLM offline`
4. `Web development without nodejs npm`
5. `Autonomous AI agent engineering`
6. `System architecture and determinism`
7. `Self-hosted cloud infrastructure`

### BISAC Categories
Pick the most specific categories so you can rank #1 in a niche, rather than #5,000 in a general category.
- `COMPUTERS / System Administration / Distributed Systems`
- `COMPUTERS / Artificial Intelligence / General`
- `COMPUTERS / Software Development & Engineering / Systems Analysis & Design`

## 5. The Series Structure
If you have a lot of content, break it down into volumes and link them as a **Series** in KDP. This puts all your books on a single Amazon series page, encouraging bulk buys.

*Example: The Daxini Stack 5-Volume Series:*
1. **The Local-First Manifesto:** Why centralized cloud servers are a trap.
2. **AI Systems Architecture & Evolution:** Deterministic execution and sovereign nodes.
3. **The Zayvora Playbook:** Operating autonomous engineering engines.
4. **Ship First:** Product deployment, knowledge artifacts, and distribution loops.
5. **Advanced Sovereign AI:** The future of portable knowledge and multi-agent swarms.

## 6. The Book Description
Your description must read like a sales letter. Highlight the **Pain** (dependency hell, vendor lock-in), the **Solution** (your architecture), and the **Contents** (what they will learn). 

*Example hook:* "Stop renting your infrastructure. Start owning your intelligence. The cloud is a trap. This is a brutal, pragmatic breakdown of a production-grade ecosystem built entirely without npm dependency hell, cloud vendor lock-in, or centralized APIs."
